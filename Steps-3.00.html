Executive Summary 3



The amount of time it takes to become a certified Tableau developer can vary depending on your existing skills and experience. However, most Tableau certification candidates typically spend several weeks to several months preparing for the exam.
Tableau offers various courses for beginners to advanced level. You should choose the course that matches your skill level and desired outcome. On average, it is recommended to dedicate 2-3 hours per day to study and practice using Tableau, and you can spread out your studies over several weeks or months.
You are on the part to taking the certification exam when you are comfortable with Tableau's core functionalities, such as data preparation, data visualization, and dashboard creation. You should also feel confident in answering practice exam questions and have a good understanding of the exam format and requirements.
Some learning strategies you can use to prepare for the certification include:
  • Practice using Tableau as much as possible to get familiar with the software's interface and functionalities.
  • Take Tableau courses that match your skill level and desired outcome, such as the Desktop Specialist, Desktop Qualified Associate, or Server Qualified Associate.
  • Use Tableau's official study materials, such as the exam study guides, sample questions, and knowledge base articles.
  • Participate in online forums and user groups to get advice and tips from other Tableau users.
  • Take practice exams to identify knowledge gaps and areas where you need to improve.
By using these strategies and dedicating time to study and practice, you can increase your chances of passing the certification exam and becoming a certified Tableau developer.
